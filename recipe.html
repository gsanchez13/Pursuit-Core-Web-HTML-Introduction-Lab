Perfect Spam Musubi

The Perfect Spam Musubi



Description

Spam musubi is a traditional Hawaiian dish, created in the 1940's during WWII by American soliders who were short on food.
They needed simple ingredients and in turn, used spam, rice and seaweed to create a now favorite delicacy found on all the islands
of Hawai'i.

Ingredients

  • 1 can of Spam
  • 2 cups of sushi rice
  • 3 sheets of nori seaweed
  • 3 cups of water
  • 2 tbsp of sugar
  • 1 tbsp of Siracha (to preference)
  • 1 tbsp of Oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp of rice vinegar
  • Plastic wrap


Directions

  1. Follow directions on sushi rice to cook while preparing the rest of the ingredients. Once rice is
    done, mix in rice vinegar and allow to cool before handling it.
  2. Cut up Spam into 8 pieces and place on medium heat skillet. Do not throw away Spam can.
  3. Add sugar, Siracha and Oyster sauce to spam and turn over once golden brown. Be careful not to
    allow sugar to burn. Remove Spam from skillet.
  4. Cut up the nori with scissors in 2x4 inch pieces.
  5. Place a piece of plastic wrap over Spam can (about 5x5 inches) and fill about 1.5 inches up with
    cooled sushi rice. Then, place cooked Spam over the rice and press onto it firmly.
  6. Pull out rice and Spam from can and remove from plastic carefully. Wrap the nori strips around the
    the Spam/rice block and completely wrap in plastic. Allow these blocks to sit for at least 15 mins.
  7. They are ready to eat! Keu a ka'ono
